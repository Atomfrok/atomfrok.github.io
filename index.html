You must have felt panicked when your cellphone or smartphone was lost. Actually this is a natural thing because in this day and age smartphones are very important objects for everyday life. But now you don't need to worry and feel panic if your smartphone is lost. Have you ever heard of the Find My Device feature? What is Find My Device? This feature can help you easily and quickly track the whereabouts of your smartphone.


The Find My Device feature functions to track and find the position of a lost cell phone. This method is effectively implemented because the process and settings are quite easy, so that the owner can find the cellphone again in a short time, less than 10 minutes. Here's how to track a cell phone using the Find My Device feature.


How to Enable the Find My Device Feature


To find out how to track a cell phone with this feature, you need to make sure of the following according to the instructions quoted from Google Support:


  • Make sure the cellphone or device to be tracked is turned on
  • Make sure you're signed in to a Google Account
  • Make sure the cellphone or device is connected to a Google account and is in active mobile data or Wi-Fi
  • Make sure Google Play is active
  • Make sure the GPS feature on the cellphone or device is active
  • Make sure the Find My Device service is active


 How to Use the Find My Device Feature


After you have successfully ensured that the Find My Device feature can be activated, then the next steps to use the Find My Device feature are as follows:


  • Go to android.com/find via a browser service and sign in to your Google Account. If you have more than one phone, then at the top of the screen click on the missing phone.


  • The lost phone will get a notification. Then from maps or maps, you will get information about the location of the lost cellphone. If the phone can't be found, you'll see its last known location, if available.


  • Next, you can choose what you will do with this Find My Device feature. You can play sound. This way, the phone will ring at maximum volume for 5 minutes, even if the device is set to silent or vibrate mode.


  • You can secure your device by locking your phone with a PIN, pattern or password. Then to help someone get their phone back, you can add a message or phone number to the lock screen.


  • Alternatively, you can wipe the device. This way you will delete all the data on the phone permanently (but not the SD card). However, after you delete the data, the Find My Device feature will not work on the cellphone.

Reducing sugar intake is beneficial not only for people who want to prevent diabetes, but also for diabetics themselves, as this is one way to control blood sugar levels. Here are tips to reduce sugar consumption for diabetics!


Tips for Reducing Sugar Consumption for Diabetics

These things are part of the tips to reduce sugar consumption for diabetics:



1. Check Added Sugar Levels on Food Labels


It is important to distinguish natural sugars (which are found naturally in food) from added sugars. Fruit, vegetables, whole grains and dairy products all contain natural sugars.



Adding sugar to the processing of a food increases the amount of calories and carbohydrates in the food consumed, which can cause health problems.



2. Choose Natural Foods, not Processed Foods


While checking food labels is important for reducing sugar intake, it's better to eat foods that don't contain added sugar, namely natural foods, such as:


  • Fruits and vegetables
  • Whole grains
  • Lean meat, fish and seafood
  • Egg
  • Dairy products (low fat or skim)

 

3. Quality Breakfast


The average Indonesian breakfast is high in sugar and carbohydrates which can increase blood sugar levels. Try consuming a breakfast that is healthier and lower in sugar or even has no added sugar at all, for example whole grain bread or whole grain bread with eggs and avocado.

Flipe healthy

4. Avoid Consuming Sweet Drinks


Drinks such as soda, sports drinks, juice, coffee with added sugar and other drinks high in sugar should be avoided. Reducing sugary drinks can significantly lower sugar intake. Diabetics should drink water, tea without sugar or coffee without sugar.



5. Replace sugar when making cakes


If you want to reduce your daily sugar intake, then reducing your intake of sweet pastries needs to be done. However, this does not mean that Diabestfriends cannot eat cake at all.

 


As an alternative to sweet cakes, make cakes with alternative recipes that use safe sugar substitutes. Try asking your doctor about safe sugar substitutes for Diabestfriends. It should also be noted that some artificial sweeteners, such as aspartame and saccharin, are not suitable for making cakes. However, stevia may be suitable for baking.

 


6. Use Natural Spices


Try making dishes or cakes using natural spices instead of table sugar. Examples of natural spices such as vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and others. Replacing sugar with natural spices can give a unique and delicious taste too, you know, so Diabestfriends can still enjoy it.

 


7. Eat Dark Chocolate


Dark chocolate is generally lower in sugar than milk chocolate or white chocolate. Dark chocolate still contains sugar, but this is the best choice if Diabestfriends are craving chocolate. But it's best to first consult with your doctor about safe portions for Diabestfriends

Did you know that gadgets such as cellphones, laptops, tablets, and others that we use for daily activities actually produce blue light or blue light which is harmful to health, especially eye health.

Blue light should be watched out for because it can have a negative effect on sleep patterns and cause various eye diseases. Here's the full explanation.

What is Blue Light?
According to eye health science, blue light or blue light is visible light with a short wavelength, around 415 to 455 nm and has a high energy level. The biggest natural source of this blue light is the sun. Apart from the sun, blue light can also come from various digital screens, including:

  • Television,
  • Gadgets or cellphones,
  • laptop Screen,
  • And other electronic equipment.
 
It should also be noted that during the day, humans are often exposed to blue light from sunlight. Blue light during the day has the benefit of improving one's mood.

Impact of Blue Light on Eye Health
What is the impact of blue light on the eyes? Blue light or blue light becomes something that is harmful to eye health when a person is exposed to too much exposure that comes from electronic device screens at night.

The various effects of blue light or blue light that you should be aware of, are as follows:

1. Impairs circadian rhythms

Excessive blue light at night can cause a decrease in the production of the hormone melatonin, the hormone responsible for regulating a person's sleep cycle.

Normally, a person's body produces a small amount of the hormone melatonin during the day, then the amount increases at certain times. The hormone melatonin will reach its peak production at midnight.

Exposure to too much blue light at night will result in a delayed sleep schedule.

According to the National Institute of General Medical Sciences, changes in circadian rhythms or cycles can cause sleep disturbances and can lead to chronic illnesses, such as obesity, depression, and bipolar disorder.



2. Causes damage to the retina

The human eye does not have sufficient protection against exposure to blue light, both from sunlight and from electronic equipment, such as gadgets.

A study from Harvard University states that blue light has long been identified as the most harmful light for the retina and can cause long-term effects in the form of damage.

Disorders that will be experienced when exposed to excessive gadget light, have a high risk of getting: glaucoma, macular degeneration and degenerative retinal disease.

3. Increased risk of cataracts

Please note that the lens of the eye is able to filter out short-wave light easily. This can protect the retina from eye damage caused by blue light or blue light.

However, while the lens is providing a protective effect for the retina, it actually suffers from decreased discoloration, which results in the formation of cataracts. Sun exposure is also a risk factor for cataracts.

If you are exposed to blue light too often from gadgets or other electronic devices, you may be more at risk of experiencing a decrease in the function of the eye lens, making it easier for you to develop cataracts.

4. Causing eye fatigue

Nowadays, most people spend their time in front of gadget screens. This activity can cause a condition known as eye fatigue or what is known as digital eye strain which can affect a person's productivity. Symptoms caused by digital eye strain include:

- Hard to focus,

- Vision gets blurry,

- Irritation and dry eyes,

- Headache, and Neck


Apart from the distance between the eyes and the screen and the time it is used, the blue light transferred by the gadget screen also plays an active role as a factor for eye fatigue.

Ways To Reduce The Impact Of Blue Light On Gadgets


When exposure to blue light from gadgets, tablets, computers or laptops already interferes with eye health, there are several ways you can do to reduce it, including the following.

  • Use anti-radiation glasses to block blue light or blue light.
  • Reduce screen-time to rest your eyes.
  • Instead of using blue light, it can be replaced with a dim red light at night.
  • Install anti-radiation on the gadget screen.
  • Turn off electronic devices before going to bed and turn off lights at bedtime.
